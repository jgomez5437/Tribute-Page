Martin Luther King Jr.

A man who stood up for the rights of millions.

Here's a timeline of Rev. King Jr.'s life:

  • 1929 - Was born in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • 1940 - King was enrolled at the Atlanta University Laboratory School for the 7th grade.
  • 1942 - When King was 13 years old, he became the youngest assistant manager of a newspaper delivery station for the Atlanta Journal.
  • 1955 - Oversaw the Montgomery bus boycott. Also became the first president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference.
  • 1963 - He helped organize multiple nonviolent protests in Birmingham, Alabama.
  • 1963 - Delivered his "I Have a Dream" speech on the steps of the Lincoln Memorial.
  • 1963 - The FBI made King an object of the FBI's COINTELPRO and investigated him for possible communist ties.
  • 1964 - King won the Nobel Peace Prize for combating racial inequality through nonviolent resistance.
  • 1965 - He helped organize two of the three Selma to Montgomery marches.
  • 1968 - Was assassinated by gunshot in Mepmphis, Tennessee.
  • 1971 - Martin Luther King Jr. Day was established as a holiday in cities and states.
  • 1977 - King was posthumously awarded the Presedential Medal of Freedom.
  • 2003 - King was also awarded the Congressional Gold Medal.
